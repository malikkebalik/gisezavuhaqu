Who is Mase?

Mase, whose real name is Mason Durell Betha, is an American rapper known for his smooth style and positive lyrics. Born on August 27, 1975, in Jacksonville, Florida, he started rapping as a teenager and was part of the group Children of the Corn in the early 1990s. Mase gained fame with Bad Boy Records, founded by Sean “Puff Daddy” Combs, in 1996. His debut single, “Feel So Good,” topped the charts, and his first album, “Harlem World,” went quadruple platinum.

After a successful run in the late ’90s, Mase surprised many by retiring from rap in 1999 to pursue a career in ministry. He made a comeback in 2004 but didn’t reach the same level of success. Despite this, Mase remains influential, shaping late ’90s hip-hop with his melodic flow and uplifting lyrics.

Who is Lil Kim?

Lil’ Kim, born Kimberly Denise Jones on July 11, 1974, in Brooklyn, New York, is a prominent American rapper, songwriter, and record executive. Recognized as one of the most influential female rappers, Lil’ Kim is known for her bold lyrics, flamboyant persona, and impactful fashion sense. Her career took off in the ’90s when she joined Junior M.A.F.I.A., led by The Notorious B.I.G.

Her debut album, “Hard Core” (1996), was a massive success, featuring hits like “No Time” and “Big Momma Thang.” Lil’ Kim continued her success with albums like “The Notorious KIM” (2000) and “La Bella Mafia” (2003). She made history in 2001 by being the first female rapper to top the Billboard Hot 100 with the “Lady Marmalade” remix.

Despite controversies, Lil’ Kim’s impact on hip-hop and popular culture is undeniable. Beyond music, she’s a successful businesswoman with her clothing line, Lil’ Kim Fashion, and her record label, Queen Bee Entertainment.
