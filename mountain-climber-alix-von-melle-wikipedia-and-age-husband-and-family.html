Mountain Climber Alix von Melle Wikipedia And Age

Alix von Melle is a talented German high-altitude mountaineer born in Hamburg on September 1, 1971.

She is the most successful female climber in German history, having ascended seven of the world’s eight-thousanders without the assistance of artificial oxygen.

After completing her studies, Alix worked at the Bavarian State Association of the German Alpine Club office for several years.

Currently, she is employed in the Munich Globetrotter branch’s press and public relations department.

Alix resided in Höhenkirchen-Siegertsbrunn and married renowned mountaineer Luis Stitzinger until his tragic passing in 2023.

Her journey into mountaineering began through ski mountaineering, eventually leading her to become a specialist instructor in mountaineering.

In 2001, she became the first German woman to climb Aconcagua (6,961m) via the challenging south-face route.

She further added to her achievements by conquering Muztagata (7,509m) in 2002, Ama Dablam (6,814m) and Huascaran Norte (6,652m) in 2004, Mount McKinley (6,190m) in 2005, Lenin Peak (7,134m) in 2007.

Alix attempted Makalu (8,485m) in 2010 but had to turn back due to extreme cold. However, she successfully scaled Cho Oyu (8,188m) later that year.

In 2011, Alix conquered Broad Peak (8,051m) via the west ridge and west flank.

She also achieved ascents of Shishapangma (8,027m) in 2012 via the north ridge and Manaslu (8,163m) twice, using the northeast flank and ridge.

Alix von Melle Husband Luis Stitzinger

Alix von Melle’s husband, Luis Stitzinger, played a significant role in her mountaineering journey.

They shared a deep love for the mountains and embarked on numerous adventures together. Living in Füssen, in the Ostallgäu region, Alix and Luis made their home surrounded by natural beauty and mountains.

They cherished their time together, and whenever the opportunity arose, they would explore the mountains, engage in mountaineering, climbing, and embark on exhilarating ski tours.

Tragically, Luis Stitzinger’s life was cut short during a tour in the Himalayas.

After being reported missing on the eight-thousander Kangchenjunga, a search team composed of Sherpas discovered his lifeless body at an altitude of 8,400 meters.

The news of his passing shook the mountaineering community and left Alix von Melle in mourning.

In an Instagram post, Alix bid a heartfelt farewell to her beloved husband.

She expressed how the mountains were his life and theirs, emphasizing their shared passion and dedication for mountaineering.

The Kangchenjunga was a lifelong dream for Luis, and his eyes would light up enthusiastically whenever he spoke about it.

The couple had spent 25 years together, and Alix expressed her gratitude for their shared time, describing them as the best years of her life.

She expressed profound sadness for the dreams they had yet to fulfill and the adventures they had planned.

Alix von Melle Family

Alix von Melle was born into a close-knit family. She grew up in Ahrensburg as the third of four children in a family of educators and businessmen.

She pursued her higher education in geography, studying in Hamburg and later at the Ludwig Maximilians University in Munich.

Her parents, a vocational school teacher and a businessman, raised her in a nurturing environment.

Despite the tragedy of her husband’s death, her family’s closeness remains strong, and they continue to support and encourage one other in their endeavors.

With seven successful ascents of the renowned eight-thousanders, her achievements highlight her exceptional skill and determination.
